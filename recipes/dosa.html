Dosa

A dosa is a thin, savoury crepe in South Indian cuisine made from a fermented batter of
ground white gram and rice. Dosas are served hot, often with chutney and sambar.
Dosas are popular in South Asia as well as around the world

Ingredents
  • rice
  • urad
  • dal
  • salt
  • water
  1. Prepare Batter: Grind soaked rice and urad dal, mix with salt, and ferment overnight.
  2. Heat Tava: Heat a non-stick dosa tava or skillet.
  3. Pour Batter: Ladle batter onto hot tava, spread in a circular motion.
  4. Add Oil: Drizzle oil or ghee around the edges of the dosa.
  5. Cook: Fry until edges lift and bottom is golden brown.
  6. Serve: Fold or roll dosa, serve hot with chutney or sambar
